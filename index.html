LAN3107 Essential Workplace Communication
Effective Oral Interaction

Structure of Workplace Meetings — Revision Reference

💡 Tip: Click a numbered tab above to jump to that stage of the meeting.
📋 1. Opening the Meeting
1. Welcoming participants
  • Welcome to the meeting, everybody. Thank you for joining today. Shall we begin? 欢迎大家参加会议。感谢各位今天的参与。我们开始吧？
  • Welcome, everybody. Thanks for attending today's meeting. I think we can start now. 欢迎大家。感谢出席今天的会议。我想我们现在可以开始了。
2. Stating the purpose of the meeting
  • The objective of our meeting today is to … 我们今天会议的目的是……
    Example
    The objective of our meeting today is to finalise the budget for next quarter. 我们今天会议的目的是确定下个季度的预算。
  • The purpose of the meeting here today is to … 今天会议的目的是……
    Example
    The purpose of the meeting here today is to discuss our new eco-friendly store policy. 今天会议的目的是讨论我们新的环保店铺政策。
  • Today, we are going to discuss … 今天，我们将讨论……
    Example
    Today, we are going to discuss ways to reduce our carbon footprint in the office. 今天，我们将讨论减少办公室碳足迹的方法。
3. Checking if participants have a copy of the agenda
  • Have you all got a copy of the agenda? 大家都有议程的副本吗？
  • Does everyone have a copy of the agenda? 每个人都有议程的副本吗？
4. Starting the meeting
  • So, let's begin with our first agenda item … 那么，让我们开始第一个议程项目……
    Example
    So, let's begin with our first agenda item, which is the new recycling programme. 那么，让我们开始第一个议程项目，即新的回收计划。
  • Let's begin by talking about … 让我们从讨论……开始
    Example
    Let's begin by talking about the feedback we received from our customers last month. 让我们从讨论上个月收到的客户反馈开始。
  • We are going to cover a few agenda items. 我们将涵盖几个议程项目。
    Example
    We are going to cover three agenda items: staffing, budget, and the new product launch. 我们将涵盖三个议程项目：人员配置、预算和新产品发布。
5. Inviting an opinion
  • I would like to hear your opinions on this topic. 我想听听大家对这个话题的看法。
  • What's your position on this issue / idea? 你对这个问题/想法持什么立场？
  • What are your opinions on this topic? 你对这个话题有什么看法？
6. Expressing an opinion
  • I strongly suggest / recommend … 我强烈建议/推荐……
    Example
    I strongly recommend we switch to reusable packaging. It will save costs in the long run. 我强烈建议我们改用可重复使用的包装。从长远来看可以节省成本。
  • Why don't we …? 我们为什么不……？
    Example
    Why don't we run a trial for three months and then review the results? 我们为什么不试行三个月，然后评估结果呢？
  • Perhaps we should … 也许我们应该……
    Example
    Perhaps we should look at what our competitors are doing before making a final decision. 也许我们应该先看看竞争对手在做什么，然后再做最终决定。
Example for opening a meeting
Good morning, everyone. Welcome to today's meeting. Let's start now. Does everyone have a copy of the agenda? [pause] The purpose of today's meeting is to discuss green measures to be implemented in the stores. We are going to cover two agenda items: ways to make our retail shops more eco-friendly and ways to promote our sustainable image to the community. Let's begin by talking about ways to make our retail shops more eco-friendly.大家早上好。欢迎参加今天的会议。我们现在开始。每个人都有议程的副本吗？今天会议的目的是讨论将在门店实施的环保措施。我们将涵盖两个议程项目：使零售店更环保的方法以及向社区推广可持续形象的方法。让我们从讨论使零售店更环保的方法开始。
💡 2. Making Suggestions
Firm and Strong Tentative
  • I strongly recommend … 我强烈建议……
    Example
    I strongly recommend we switch to reusable packaging. It will save costs in the long run. 我强烈建议我们改用可重复使用的包装。从长远来看可以节省成本。
  • I suggest … 我建议……
    Example
    I suggest organising a staff workshop on sustainability before the end of the month. 我建议在月底前组织一次员工可持续发展研讨会。
  • In my opinion, we should … 在我看来，我们应该……
    Example
    In my opinion, we should invest in solar panels for our main store. 在我看来，我们应该为主要店铺投资太阳能板。
  • In my view, the only solution is … 依我看，唯一的解决办法是……
    Example
    In my view, the only solution is to reduce plastic use across all departments. 依我看，唯一的解决办法是在所有部门减少塑料使用。
  • Let's … 让我们……
    Example
    Let's set a clear deadline so that everyone knows what to prepare. 让我们设定一个明确的截止日期，这样每个人都知道该准备什么。
  • How about …? ……怎么样？
    Example
    How about conducting a workshop on risk management as our coming staff development activity? 举办一次风险管理研讨会作为我们即将进行的员工发展活动怎么样？
  • It might be a good idea to … ……可能是个好主意。
    Example
    It might be a good idea to survey our customers first to understand their preferences. 先调查一下我们的客户以了解他们的偏好可能是个好主意。
  • May I suggest …? 我可以建议……吗？
    Example
    May I suggest we invite an environmental consultant to advise us on best practices? 我可以建议我们邀请一位环境顾问来指导我们最佳做法吗？
  • Perhaps we should / could … 也许我们应该/可以……
    Example
    Perhaps we should look at what our competitors are doing before making a final decision. 也许我们应该先看看竞争对手在做什么，然后再做最终决定。
  • Shall we …? 我们……好吗？
    Example
    Shall we put this idea to a vote so we can move forward together? 我们把想法投票表决，以便共同推进好吗？
  • What about …? ……怎么样？
    Example
    What about introducing a rewards scheme for customers who bring their own bags? 为自带袋子的顾客推出奖励计划怎么样？
  • What if …? 如果……呢？
    Example
    What if we replace all plastic cups in the office with reusable ones? 如果我们把办公室所有的塑料杯都换成可重复使用的呢？
  • Why don't we …? 我们为什么不……？
    Example
    Why don't we run a trial for three months and then review the results? 我们为什么不试行三个月，然后评估结果呢？
  • Would it be suitable / possible to …? ……是否合适/可能？
    Example
    Would it be possible to get the data before our next meeting so we can be better prepared? 是否可以在下次会议之前拿到数据，以便我们能更好地准备？
  • I am wondering if we could … 我在想我们是否能够……
    Example
    I am wondering if we could partner with a local charity to promote our green image. 我在想我们是否能够与当地慈善机构合作，推广我们的绿色形象。
💬 3. Responding to Suggestions
Agreement Partial Agreement Disagreement
  • Yes, sure. 是的，当然。
  • I think so. 我也这么认为。
  • That's what I'm going to say. 这正是我想说的。
  • You're right. 你说得对。
  • I agree. 我同意。
  • Absolutely. 完全同意。
  • That's a good idea. 这是个好主意。
  • I can't agree with you more. 我非常同意你。
  • That's an option, but … 这是一个选择，但是……
    Example
    That's an option, but I think we should consider rescheduling instead of cancelling.
    这是一个选择，但我认为我们应该考虑重新安排而不是取消。
  • It's a possibility, but … 这是一种可能性，但是……
    Example
    It's a possibility, but we need to think about the extra costs involved.
    这是一种可能性，但我们需要考虑涉及到的额外成本。
  • Well, I see your point, but … 嗯，我明白你的意思，但是……
    Example
    Well, I see your point, but extending the deadline might delay the whole project.
    嗯，我明白你的意思，但延长截止日期可能会延误整个项目。
  • I'm afraid I can't go along with you. 恐怕我不能同意你的看法。
  • I don't think we could … 我不认为我们可以……
    Example
    I don't think we could finish the whole report by tomorrow. We need at least three days.
    我不认为我们能在明天之前完成整个报告。我们至少需要三天。
  • Sorry, I don't quite see it the same way. 抱歉，我不太认同。
  • I don't quite agree with you. 我不太同意你的看法。
  • Sorry, that may not be possible. 抱歉，那可能不行。
  • I'm not sure about that. 我不太确定。
Examples on making suggestions and responding to suggestions

Tracy: I suggest that we should reduce our paper usage. For example, we could replace paper bags with reusable or biodegradable alternatives. Do you think that's a good idea?

Chris: That's a great idea! We should also encourage customers to bring their own bags. Maybe we can offer a small discount for those who do …

Dave: Can we all agree that we should present these options to the management team?

Tracy: Sure. Let's do that.

Dave: Excellent! Let's move on to the second agenda item. What are your opinions on the ways to promote our sustainable image to the community? (Discussion continues)

Tracy: 我建议我们应该减少纸张使用。例如，我们可以用可重复使用或可生物降解的替代品来取代纸袋。你觉得这是个好主意吗？
Chris: 好主意！我们还应该鼓励顾客自带袋子。也许我们可以为这样做的人提供小额折扣……
Dave: 我们是否都同意应该把这些方案提交给管理团队？
Tracy: 当然。我们就这样做。
Dave: 太好了！让我们继续第二个议程项目。你对向社区推广可持续形象的方法有什么看法？（讨论继续）
🙋 4. Keeping the Discussion Going
Interrupting Politely interrupt a speaker when you have something to add
  • Excuse me, could I add something here? 打扰一下，我能在这里补充一点吗？
  • May I jump in for a moment? 我可以插一句话吗？
Asking for clarification If you do not fully understand a point, ask for clarification
  • Could you please clarify what you mean by …? 你能澄清一下你说的……是什么意思吗？
    Example
    Could you please clarify what you mean by "proactive"? Are you suggesting we start before the deadline?
    你能澄清一下你说的"积极主动"是什么意思吗？你是建议我们在截止日期之前开始吗？
  • Can you give an example of …? 你能举一个……的例子吗？
    Example
    Can you give an example of what a flexible approach would look like in practice?
    你能举一个例子说明灵活的方法在实践中是怎样的吗？
Asking for contributions Engage all members by asking for their thoughts
  • I'd like to hear your thoughts on this, [Name]. 我想听听你对此的看法，[姓名]。
  • [Name], what do you think about this idea? [姓名]，你觉得这个想法怎么样？
  • I would like to hear your opinions on this topic. 我想听听你对此话题的看法。
  • What's your position on this issue? 你对这个问题持什么立场？
⚖️ 5. Discussing & Evaluating Options
Stating Preference / Advocating Your Ideas Evaluating Suggestions
  • I (would) prefer (not) … 我（更）宁愿（不）……
    Example
    I would prefer not to make any changes until we have seen the full data.
    我更愿意在看到完整数据之前不做任何更改。
  • I would rather (not) … 我宁愿（不）……
    Example
    I would rather not commit to a date until we have spoken to all the stakeholders.
    我宁愿在跟所有利益相关者谈过之前不确定日期。
  • My preference is … 我的偏好是……
    Example
    My preference is to roll out the new system gradually rather than all at once.
    我的偏好是逐步推出新系统，而不是一次性全部推行。
  • From my perspective, … is better. 从我的角度来看，……更好。
    Example
    From my perspective, option A is better because it is more cost-effective in the long run.
    从我的角度来看，选项A更好，因为从长远来看它更具成本效益。
  • However, … 然而，……
    Example
    The new plan sounds exciting. However, we need to think carefully about the additional costs.
    新计划听起来令人兴奋。然而，我们需要仔细考虑额外成本。
  • Although … / Whereas … / While … 虽然……/然而……/尽管……
    Example
    Although the idea is creative, I think we need more time to plan the details properly.
    虽然这个想法很有创意，但我认为我们需要更多时间来妥善规划细节。
  • …, but … ……, 但是……
    Example
    The proposal looks promising, but we need to consider the impact on our current budget.
    这个提案看起来很有前景，但我们需要考虑对我们当前预算的影响。
  • On the one hand, … / On the other hand, … 一方面，……/另一方面，……
    Example
    On the one hand, expanding into the online market could significantly increase our revenue. On the other hand, it would require substantial investment in logistics and customer support.
    一方面，拓展在线市场可以显著增加我们的收入。另一方面，这需要在物流和客户支持方面进行大量投资。
🤝 6. Reaching a Decision
Recognising Common Ground Acknowledging the Need to Compromise
  • It seems that we can all agree that … 看来我们都同意……
    Example
    It seems that we can all agree that reducing plastic use is the right thing to do.
    看来我们都同意减少塑料使用是正确的做法。
  • It seems that we have a consensus that … 看来我们达成了共识……
    Example
    It seems that we have a consensus that the event should be held in the second quarter.
    看来我们达成了共识，活动应该在第二季度举行。
  • We all concur that … 我们都同意……
    Example
    We all concur that further research is needed before we commit to a final plan.
    我们都同意在确定最终计划之前需要进一步研究。
  • We are all in alignment that … 我们都一致认为……
    Example
    We are all in alignment that customer satisfaction must remain our top priority.
    我们都一致认为客户满意度必须保持为我们的首要任务。
  • It seems we have differing views, but maybe we could find a middle ground. 看来我们有不同意见，但也许我们可以找到一个折中方案。
  • We don't quite agree on every point, so let's explore options that we could all support. 我们不是在每个点上都完全同意，所以让我们探讨一下我们都能支持的选择。
  • It seems like we have different options, so let's see if we meet in the middle. 看来我们有不同的选择，让我们看看是否能折中一下。
Compromising Revisiting the Agenda in the Next Meeting
  • On second thoughts, your suggestion may be more cost-effective / workable / practical. Let's make it our final decision then. 再想想，你的建议可能更具成本效益/可行/实用。那我们就把它作为最终决定吧。
  • On further reflection, your idea seems more practical / sensible / feasible. I can go along with that. 进一步反思后，你的想法似乎更实际/明智/可行。我可以同意。
  • It seems like we don't have time to make a decision today. Let's discuss the item again in our next meeting. 看来我们今天没有时间做决定了。让我们在下一次会议上再讨论这个项目。
  • Let's revisit the agenda item again in the next meeting. 让我们在下一次会议上重新讨论这个议程项目。
  • Rather than rushing to make a decision now, shall we revisit this item in our next meeting? 与其现在匆忙做决定，我们不如在下一次会议上重新讨论这个项目？
  • We can't reach a decision today, so let's carry this conversation over to our next meeting. 我们今天无法达成决定，所以让我们把这个讨论延续到下一次会议。
✅ 7. Closing the Meeting
Signalling the end of the meeting
  • It looks like we've run out of time, so I guess we'll finish here. 看来我们时间不够了，我想我们就到此为止吧。
  • I think that's all we have time for today. Let's talk more about this next time. 我想我们今天就到这里。下次我们再详谈。
  • I think we've covered everything on the agenda. 我想我们已经涵盖了议程上的所有内容。
Checking for any other business
  • Is there any other business? 还有其他事项吗？
  • Does anyone have any other business they would like to discuss? 有没有人还有其他事项想要讨论？
Summarising the discussion
  • To summarise, we've agreed to … 总结一下，我们同意……
    Example
    To summarise, we've agreed to launch the pilot scheme in Store A starting next month.
    总结一下，我们同意从下个月开始在A店启动试点计划。
  • Let me run through the main points we've agreed on. 让我梳理一下我们达成一致的主要要点。
  • So, to conclude, we've agreed that … 那么，总之，我们同意……
Thanking everyone for their participation
  • I think we've had a very productive meeting today and covered a lot of ground. Thank you so much for your input. 我认为我们今天开了一个非常富有成效的会议，涵盖了很多内容。非常感谢大家的意见。
  • We've had a very fruitful meeting today and discussed a lot of issues. I would like to say thank you for your participation. 我们今天开了一个非常富有成效的会议，讨论了很多问题。我想对大家的参与表示感谢。
  • This has been an extremely effective meeting today, everybody. Thank you very much for all your suggestions. 今天这次会议非常高效。非常感谢大家的所有建议。
Example for closing a meeting

A / B / C / D: Well, I think that's all we have time for today. We have some really promising ideas to take to management. Does anybody have anything else to contribute?

All: No. / I don't. / Not right now.

A / B / C / D: Well, if nobody has anything else to add, I think we can finish here. Thank you very much for attending the meeting and making contributions, everybody.

A / B / C / D: 好了，我想今天的时间就到这里。我们有一些非常有前景的想法可以提交给管理层。还有人要补充吗？
All: 没有了。/ 我没有。/ 现在没有。
A / B / C / D: 好吧，如果没有人要补充了，我想我们可以到此结束。非常感谢大家参加会议并做出贡献。
