Learning objectives

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:

  • identify the essential elements of a personal story
  • describe the 'moment of realisation' in personal stories
  • identify the key components of a self-introduction
  • apply storytelling techniques in job interviews
  • demonstrate effective non-verbal communication skills in job interviews

The components of a personal story

A strong personal story usually flows through four key components in this order:

Step 1
Setting
Where and when the story takes place — the context and background.
→
Step 2
Struggle
The challenge or difficulty the storyteller faced.
→
Step 3
Realisation
The "Aha" moment — a turning point or key insight.
→
Step 4
Solution
How the storyteller acted on the insight and what they achieved.

    Task 2 — Moment of realisation in personal stories

    For personal stories, there is usually a moment of realisation where the storyteller gains insights into their experiences, becomes aware of something significant, and transforms their understanding of themselves or their situation. Include that moment — in addition to the 3 S's — to emphasise the key insight or turning point.

    Task 3 — Storytelling in job interviews

    Sharing personal stories in a job interview is an effective way to make your responses more memorable and help you stand out from other candidates. You may apply storytelling techniques to introduce yourself or answer interview questions about your interest in the position, company, or industry.
